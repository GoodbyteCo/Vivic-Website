Vivic Research partnered with the Centre for Resilience and Social Development (CRSD) and the Ottawa Coalition to End Violence Against Women (OCTEVAW) to explore how systemic and institutional responses fail racialized, immigrant, refugee, and non-status survivors of gender-based violence. This work identifies what transformative responses require to ensure that they do not replicate the same shortcomings we see from mainstream legal and police-based responses. This research was conducted in direct response to the growing calls for alternatives to legal and police-based reporting tools and responses. The research engaged directly with community members with lived experience of precarious immigration, frontline workers, and advocates through interviews and focus groups.
